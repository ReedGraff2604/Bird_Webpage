Barn Owls

The barn owls are the most widely distributed group of owls in the world. They are medium-sized owls with large heads and characteristic heart-shaped faces. They have long, strong legs with powerful talons. Barn owls live in open habitats, such as grasslands, agricultural fields, and deserts, and they usually have babies between March and August.

Snowy Owls

The snowy owl, also known as the polar owl, the white owl and the Arctic owl, is a large, white owl of the true owl family. Snowy owls are native to the Arctic regions of both North America and the Palearctic, breeding mostly on the tundra. Snowy owls live in the Arctic regions of North America and Eurasia, and they usually have babies in mid-June.

Great Horned Owls

The great horned owl, also known as the tiger owl or the hoot owl, is a large owl native to the Americas. It is an extremely adaptable bird with a vast range and is the most widely distributed true owl in the Americas. Great horned owls live in a wide range of habitats across North, Central, and South America, including forests, deserts, wetlands, grasslands, and more. Great horned owls usually have babies in mid-February to late March. Great horned owls have very few predators, so their eggs and babies are the ones that are usually targeted.
